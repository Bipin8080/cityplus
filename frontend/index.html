How It Works

A four-step process involving three roles — Citizen, Admin, and Staff — to report, manage, and resolve civic issues.

01
person Citizen
edit_note

Submit an Issue

The citizen fills in the report form with a description, category, and location, and uploads a photo. The report is saved to the database with a status of Pending and a unique tracking ID is generated.

02
admin_panel_settings Admin
manage_search

Admin Reviews & Assigns

The administrator logs into the dashboard and reviews the submitted report. If accepted, the admin assigns the issue to an available staff member responsible for handling it.

03
engineering Staff
construction

Staff Works on Issue

The assigned staff member receives the issue and carries out the required field work. They update the status to In Progress when work begins, and to Resolved once completed.

04
person Citizen
task_alt

Issue Resolved

Once the staff marks the issue as Resolved, it is closed in the system. The citizen can view the final status through their dashboard or by entering their tracking ID.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the CityPlus platform.

How do I track my report?

expand_more

Once you submit a report, you will receive a unique tracking ID. You can use this ID in the "Track Status" section of the platform, or log in to your dashboard to see the current status of your reports.

Who can see my reported issues?

expand_more

Submitted reports are visible to the admin and the staff member assigned to the issue. Your personal contact information is kept private and is only accessible to the administrator.

How long does it take to resolve an issue?

expand_more

Resolution time depends on when the administrator reviews the report and how long the assigned staff member takes to complete the field work. You can check the current status at any time using your tracking ID.

What categories of issues can I report?

expand_more

You can report a wide range of civic issues including potholes, broken streetlights, illegal waste dumping, water leaks, noise complaints, park maintenance needs, and public property vandalism.
